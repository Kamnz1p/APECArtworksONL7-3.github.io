THE ORIGIN OF ART

What is National Arts Month? National Arts Month is celebrated in the Philippines every February. It was officially established through Presidential Proclamation No. 683, signed on January 28, 1991, by President Corazon C. Aquino. The celebration was created to recognize Filipino artistic excellence, preserve the nation’s cultural heritage, and promote the seven major art disciplines. Since then, February has been dedicated to honoring the creativity, talent, and contributions of Filipino artists. APEC Schools commemorates National Arts Month by holding weeklong activities that highlight students’ creativity and talents. These events allow learners to participate in performances and artistic presentations, making the celebration a meaningful school tradition.
